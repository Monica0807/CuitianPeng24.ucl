Effects of Resveratrol on Antioxidant Capacity and Apoptosis of Duck Liver Under Acute Heat Stress

Resveratrol (RES), a polyphenolic compound produced by plants under stress, has multiple biological effects, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-aging properties. This study investigated its impact on the antioxidant capacity and apoptosis of duck liver under acute heat stress. Resveratrol supplementation at 400 mg·kg⁻¹ improved antioxidant enzyme activity, activated the SIRT1 signaling pathway, reduced oxidative damage, and enhanced liver resilience against heat-induced stress.

Methods

120 Shanma ducks were divided into control (C) and RES groups, with RES receiving a diet supplemented with 400 mg·kg⁻¹ resveratrol. Ducks were subjected to heat stress at 39°C for 0, 30, or 60 minutes. Serum and liver samples were collected to analyze mRNA and protein levels, apoptosis markers, and antioxidant enzyme activities using qRT-PCR, Western blot, and TUNEL techniques.

Results

Resveratrol activated the SIRT1 pathway, increased anti-apoptotic gene expression, and reduced pro-apoptotic markers, improving antioxidant capacity and mitigating heat-induced liver damage.
